Exploring innovative AI applications at the intersection of computer vision, generative models, and medical imaging.

Submit Your Paper

Overview

This workshop focuses on the intersection of computer vision, generative models, and medical imaging, exploring innovative AI applications in healthcare. It aims to bring together researchers, clinicians, and industry professionals to present and discuss novel methodologies that address key challenges in medical imaging and healthcare. In particular, the workshop highlights the transformative potential of generative models and advanced computational techniques in areas such as disease progression modeling, synthetic data generation, multimodal data integration, and both imaging and non-imaging diagnostics. These approaches have the potential to revolutionize precision medicine by improving diagnostic accuracy, enhancing personalized care, and addressing data scarcity issues in clinical research.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions to the workshop session "Computer Vision and Generative Models for Medical Imaging" at ICIAP 2025. This workshop explores innovative applications of AI and computer vision in healthcare, with a focus on medical imaging and generative models.

Topics of Interest Include:

  • Novel Deep Learning Architectures for Medical Data Analysis and Synthesis: Addressing the critical need for large, diverse datasets in medical research.
  • Generative Models for Predictive Healthcare: Facilitating the modeling and simulation of complex health conditions, enhancing understanding, forecasting, and decision-making for improved patient outcomes.
  • Multimodal Learning Approaches: Integrating diverse data types (imaging, clinical, genetic, lifestyle) for more comprehensive patient analysis.
  • Privacy-Preserving Techniques for Healthcare Data: Crucial for maintaining patient confidentiality while advancing medical research.
  • Explainable AI Methods for Medical Image Interpretation: Enhancing trust and adoption of AI in clinical settings by providing interpretable results.
  • Clinical Applications of AI-Powered Medical Imaging Analysis: Demonstrating real-world impact of AI in improving diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) Techniques for Clinical Narratives: Unlocking valuable information from unstructured medical text data.
  • Ethical Considerations in AI-Powered Healthcare: Addressing critical concerns about bias, fairness, and responsible AI deployment in healthcare.

Submission Guidelines

Submitted papers must not exceed 12 pages, including references. Only original work that has not been previously published or concurrently submitted to other venues will be considered for inclusion in the proceedings. All submissions must be in PDF format and submitted via the EasyChair system. Accepted papers will be published in a joint post-workshop proceedings volume as part of the Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series, which is indexed in Web of Science, Scopus, EI Engineering Index, Google Scholar, DBLP, and other major databases. Authors are encouraged to prepare and submit their manuscripts using the official LNCS template (Overleaf Template).
